The verified rerun of the in_distri configuration was conducted using the same experimental setup and symbolic feature dimensionality of 35 features as the original raw-data experiment. The purpose of this rerun was to evaluate the reproducibility of the obtained results and to determine whether the previously observed performance could be replicated under a different random initialization.

The rerun achieved a best validation accuracy of 63.51% at epoch 206. In comparison, the original raw-data experiment reached a best validation accuracy of 62.00% at epoch 270. The rerun therefore improved upon the original result by approximately 1.5 percentage points while reaching its optimum performance somewhat earlier during training.

Despite this numerical improvement, the overall behavior of the model remained highly consistent across both runs. In both experiments, validation accuracy increased gradually over a long training period, reflecting the greater complexity of the in_distri configuration compared to simpler tasks such as center_single, left_right, up_down, and in_out. Unlike the distribute_four and distribute_nine configurations, which plateaued below 50% accuracy, the model was able to learn a substantially larger proportion of the underlying relational structure in the in_distri task, ultimately reaching accuracies above 60% in both runs.

The close agreement between the original experiment and the verified rerun indicates that the obtained performance is stable with respect to random initialization. Although some variation in the exact validation accuracy and convergence epoch is visible, the magnitude of these differences is relatively small compared to the overall performance level. Consequently, the observed results appear to reflect a genuine characteristic of the reproduced SCL implementation rather than stochastic variation arising from training.

Overall, the rerun confirms the conclusions drawn from the original experiment. The in_distri configuration remains one of the more challenging I-RAVEN tasks, yet the model consistently performs substantially better than on the distribute_four and distribute_nine configurations.
