The in_distri configuration used the corresponding symbolic feature dimensionality of 35 features.

The in_distri experiment was conducted using the same training setup as the other single-task configurations in order to maintain comparability across the replication study. Training was performed for 300 epochs using the Adam optimizer with a learning rate of 0.005 and a weight decay of 0.01. The training batch size was set to 128, while the evaluation batch size was set to 32. The experiment employed the raw index-based loading pipeline, where symbolic representations were generated directly from the original I-RAVEN dataset during training and evaluation.

The experiment completed successfully and achieved a best validation accuracy of 62.00% at epoch 270. Unlike several of the simpler configurations, performance improved gradually over a long period of training and continued to increase even during the later stages of optimization. The best validation accuracy was only reached near the end of training, indicating that the model required substantially more epochs to approach its maximum performance on this task. At the final epoch, validation accuracy remained close to the best recorded value, suggesting that optimization had largely converged.

The results place in_distri between the easiest and most difficult configurations of the benchmark. While the model performed substantially better than on distribute_four and distribute_nine, where validation accuracy remained below 50%, performance was considerably lower than on center_single, left_right, up_down, and in_out, all of which achieved near-perfect or perfect accuracy. This intermediate position suggests that the symbolic reasoning requirements of the in_distri configuration are more challenging than those of the directional and hierarchical tasks but less demanding than the most complex distributed reasoning configurations.

A noticeable gap remained between training and validation performance throughout the later stages of training. Training accuracy exceeded 85% by the end of optimization, whereas validation accuracy remained close to 62%. This difference indicates that the model was able to learn substantial task-specific structure from the training data but encountered greater difficulty generalizing these learned representations to unseen examples. Nevertheless, the gap was smaller than that observed for the distribute_four and distribute_nine tasks, reflecting the comparatively stronger generalization achieved on in_distri.

From a technical perspective, the experiment further confirms the stability of the adapted implementation. Training completed successfully for all 300 epochs without numerical instability, optimization failures, or implementation-related issues. The steady improvement observed throughout training provides additional evidence that the reproduced environment preserves the intended learning dynamics of the original model.

Taken together with the other raw-data experiments, the in_distri results reinforce a clear pattern across the benchmark. The replicated SCL architecture achieves near-perfect performance on several configurations characterized by comparatively constrained relational structures, while performance decreases substantially as the complexity of the required symbolic reasoning increases. The in_distri configuration occupies an intermediate position within this spectrum and therefore provides an important reference point for understanding how task structure influences model performance within the I-RAVEN benchmark.
