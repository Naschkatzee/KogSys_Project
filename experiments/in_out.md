The in_out experiment was conducted using the same technical setup as the previous experiments to maintain direct comparability across all I-RAVEN configurations. Training was performed for 300 epochs using the Adam optimizer with a learning rate of 0.005 and a weight decay of 0.01. The training batch size was set to 128, while evaluation used a batch size of 32. The experiment used the generated I-RAVEN dataset together with the previously prepared train, validation, and test index files. Logs, checkpoints, summaries, and metadata were continuously stored during execution.

The experiment completed successfully and achieved very strong final performance. After 300 epochs, the model reached approximately 99.98% training accuracy and approximately 99.85% test accuracy. The training and test losses steadily decreased throughout training and stabilized at very low values in the later epochs.

The results indicate almost no meaningful overfitting. Although the model eventually approached perfect training accuracy, the test accuracy remained extremely close to the training performance across the final epochs. Temporary fluctuations in individual epochs recovered quickly and did not produce sustained degradation. Overall, the convergence behavior remained stable and smooth throughout the full 300-epoch run.

From a reasoning perspective, the in_out configuration appears substantially easier for the reproduced SCL implementation than the dense grid-based tasks distribute_four and distribute_nine. Together with the previously successful center_single, left_right, and up_down experiments, the in_out result further strengthens the emerging pattern observed throughout the replication process. The adapted implementation performs extremely well on configurations involving simpler spatial partitions or hierarchical relations, while difficulties remain concentrated specifically in distributed multi-object grid reasoning tasks.

The experiment also provides additional evidence supporting the validity of the reconstructed training pipeline. The achieved accuracy is highly consistent with the near-perfect performance reported in the original paper for comparable configurations. Consequently, the current replication can now be considered successful across several major I-RAVEN configurations, while simultaneously highlighting a persistent discrepancy limited primarily to the more complex distributed grid-based settings.
